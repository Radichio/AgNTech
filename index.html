Vision & Mission

Why AgNtech Connect exists.

I bring serious capital — and the people who know how to deploy it — together with the founders and operators building the future of Canadian agriculture. Forty years a farmer, an operator, and an investor: my judgment is the engine behind every introduction.

Vision

To help bring the rural economy to its full, sustainable potential — here in Manitoba.

Mission

To bring together people and resources that share these values, from around the globe — building the advanced systems behind sustainable agriculture, secure food, and a clean environment. For all of humanity.

Analyze & advise

Four decades on the land and in the boardroom, put to work for the people who ask. Honest counsel comes first.

Bring in people & resources

Connecting capital, expertise, and the right partners — governments, funds, family offices, operators — to vetted opportunities here.

Build

Backing founders and building companies — as investor, director, and advisor, from concept through to exit.

On this land

This work takes place on Treaty 1 and Treaty 2 territory in Manitoba — the traditional lands of the Anishinaabe, Cree, Oji-Cree, Dakota, and Dene peoples, and the homeland of the Red River Métis. I hold that history, and the peoples who carry it, with respect.

What I do

An operating partner, invested in the outcome.

I've spent four decades farming the land and, at the same time, helping other companies — from start-up to exits — as an investor, director, or advisor. A farm I grew from 480 acres to more than 10,000. Ventures across agriculture, technology, and bioscience. A feature film, financed and delivered.

I can turn a concept into reality.

That's the work: opening the right doors, vetting hard, structuring the deal cleanly, and staying in it afterward — paid to get it right, with my own stake in the outcome.

Who I work with

Capital entering the sector

Capital, placed well.

Governments, funds, and family offices placing capital in Canadian agriculture. I source and vet the opportunities, structure the entry, and navigate the ownership and review rules that catch outside money off guard — a trusted local who has deployed capital and seen an exit.

Lenders with distressed exposure

A workout, not a write‑off.

For lenders holding agricultural exposure that has turned. I read the operation honestly and structure the workout — restructuring where there’s a path, and finding the orderly outcome that recovers more than a distressed sale. Forty years through hard cycles is the judgment behind it.
