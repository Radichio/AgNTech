Careers & talent

Bringing world-class talent to Manitoba agriculture.

I bring capital into Canadian agriculture. I’d like to do the same with people — the technologists, engineers, and operators this region needs. It’s early, and I’m building it deliberately.

Where this stands: I’m building the relationships and the routes in — not running a placement agency. If it’s a fit, whether to work with us or to build elsewhere in this region, reach out and I’ll be straight about what I can do today.
Talent is a resource, too

The same role, applied to people.

Manitoba’s agriculture and technology companies need people the wider market hasn’t sent them — and the people who would do the most interesting work here often don’t know the work exists.

Connecting the two is the same job I already do with capital. I’m early in building it, but the intent is plain: make it easier for good people to find their way to this region, and for the companies here to find them.

For those coming from away

If you’re coming from away.

Some of the right people for this work will come from outside Canada. If you’re a skilled technologist, engineer, or operator drawn to Canadian agriculture, Manitoba is worth a look. I’m still building the routes that make a move here straightforward, so I won’t overstate what’s in place yet — but the interest is genuine, and it starts with a conversation.

And we’re building our own

A lab of our own.

Alongside the advisory work, AgNtech is building a development lab — somewhere to test and prove new agriculture technology on working farmland rather than on paper. It’s where a concept has to earn its keep in the field. As it grows, so does the team around it, and that’s where our own roles open first.
